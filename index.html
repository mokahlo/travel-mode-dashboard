Break-Even & Insights

Break-even: The break-even distance is the point where two travel modes score the same on a given metric (cost, carbon, or the combined "generalized" cost). If a break-even (for example ~300 miles) is shown, one mode tends to be preferable below that distance and the other becomes preferable beyond it. "No positive break-even" means one mode dominates across plausible trip distances.
Generalized cost = monetary cost + (value-of-time × travel hours).
